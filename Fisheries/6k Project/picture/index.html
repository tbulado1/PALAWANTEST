CTE GOAL

The College of Teacher Education aims to upgrade the quality of basic education in the country by providing excellent instruction, research, extension, and production services relevant to pre-service teacher education.

OBJECTIVES

Bachelor of Physical Education

To produce scientifically inclined physical education graduates who are imbued with values and whose knowledge and skills will respond to the national and global demands. Moreover, it will prepare and train qualified students to teach Physical Education, Health Education, and Sports in the Elementary, Secondary, and Tertiary levels, and to become sports, dance, recreation, and fitness specialists.

Welcome Message

The College of Teacher Education is committed to maintain its status as the Center of Excellence in Teacher Education in MIMAROPA. Currently, the College has three (3) curricular programs, namely: the Bachelor of Secondary Education (BSED); the Bachelor of Elementary Education (BEED); and the Bachelor of Physical Education (BPED).

Since January 2023, the BSED program is ACCREDITED Level 4 while the BEED program had already compiled all requirements and is waiting for results. Hopefully, the BEED program can be granted Level 4 status this month. Today, we have the honor to submit the BPED program for this preliminary survey visit.

The Bachelor of Physical Education (BPED) is a newly implemented program at the College, designed to produce graduates who are not only physically educated but also advocates of health and wellness. Even with challenges brought by the pandemic and extreme weather, the College continued to offer the best learning experiences through blended learning modalities that allow our students to adapt and succeed. Thus, we were able to harvest the first cohort of graduates in July 2023.

The faculty, led by the Chairperson and the Dean of the College of Teacher Education, ensured that students receive the quality education they deserve, resulting in a higher passing rate for first-time LET takers since March 2023. The Department of Physical Education has also forged partnerships with local and international institutions through research, conferences, and training.

Our team has diligently prepared for this visit, ensuring all documents and evidence are in place to demonstrate our commitment to providing quality education. On behalf of the entire faculty and staff, I welcome you to CTE – the home of innovators!

Dr. Maries A. Salvador

Dean, College of Teacher Education

Welcome to the Hub of Future Fitness and Wellness Advocates, and Physical Education Teachers!

On behalf of the College of Teacher Education's Department of Physical Education, we are excited to welcome you to the Bachelor of Physical Education Preliminary Survey Visit.

Five years ago, the BPED program was introduced as a new pathway for students aspiring to become PE teachers. In 2022, the program earned a Certificate of Compliance from the Commission on Higher Education, affirming adherence to the rigorous standards of Palawan State University.

This year, we are seeking a preliminary survey visit as the initial step toward formal accreditation. We believe this will validate our commitment to quality and enhance students’ educational experience.

We are eager to share the achievements and innovations our team has worked on. We are committed to transparency and open dialogue and look forward to productive discussions that will contribute to the continuous enhancement of our program.

Ms. Diane Low J. Bautista

Chairperson, Department of Physical Education
